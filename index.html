Latest work
Job market paper

Disruption without destruction: television entry and the reorganization of commercial radio

Uses the FCC's 1948–1952 television licensing freeze as a natural experiment to study how commercial radio's business model reorganized under TV entry. Finds a compositional revenue shift from network compensation to local advertising, with heterogeneous effects by market size — large markets de-networked while small markets gained network revenue through exclusive reach to non-TV households.
